<p><p>The surgical bioadhesive market is categorized into three main types: fibrin adhesives, mussel adhesive proteins, and others. Fibrin adhesives are derived from fibrinogen and thrombin, promoting wound healing and tissue bonding. Mussel adhesive proteins mimic the natural adhesives found in mussels, providing strong adhesion in wet conditions. The "others" category includes various synthetic and natural adhesives not classified under fibrin or mussel types, offering diverse applications across surgical procedures, promoting healing, and reducing complications.</p></p>
