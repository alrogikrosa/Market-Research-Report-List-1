<p><p>Commercial photovoltaic (PV) systems are primarily categorized into two types based on the solar cell materials used: monocrystalline and polysilicon systems. Monocrystalline systems are known for their higher efficiency and space-saving design, as they are made from a single crystal structure. In contrast, polysilicon systems, formed from multiple silicon crystals, are generally less efficient but more cost-effective. Both types play a vital role in the commercial solar market, catering to different needs and budget considerations while contributing to sustainable energy solutions.</p></p>
