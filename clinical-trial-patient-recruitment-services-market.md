<p><p>Clinical trial patient recruitment services are categorized into four phases. Phase I involves healthy volunteers for safety assessments of a drug. Phase II focuses on a limited patient population to evaluate efficacy and side effects. Phase III expands the study to a larger group to confirm effectiveness and monitor adverse reactions, critical for regulatory approval. Phase IV occurs post-approval to gather additional data on long-term effects and ongoing safety, often involving diverse real-world patient populations. Each phase requires tailored recruitment strategies to meet specific objectives.</p></p>
