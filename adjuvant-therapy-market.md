<p><p>Adjuvant therapy enhances the effectiveness of primary treatment in cancer care by targeting remaining cancer cells. Chemotherapy uses drugs to kill or slow the growth of cancer cells, while radiotherapy employs targeted radiation to destroy tumors. Hormonotherapy disrupts hormone signaling that fuels certain cancers, making it effective for hormone-sensitive disorders. Other therapies may include immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and supportive care measures. Together, these are crucial in improving patient outcomes and reducing the risk of cancer recurrence.</p></p>
